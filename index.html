from: http://www.mappmycity.ca/Html5Viewer_2_0/Index.html?configBase=http://www.mappmycity.ca/Geocortex/Essentials/REST/sites/Open_Spaces_and_Parks/viewers/Viewer_HTML5_20/virtualdirectory/Resources/Config/Default The city of windsor doesn't use Google - they use a powerful software suite called a Geographic Information System But the power of that system is for those who work at the city. We just get a view of the maps they create

from: http://www.fordcity.ca/community-map/ One of the reasons why Google Maps is so popular is that it allows all of us to make our own maps This is the Ford City Community Map - which doesn't look too different than the City one
from: http://www.historypin.com/ There are a variety of websites that make the mashing up of google maps easier. Here's another example: recently some UofW History students added images to History Pin

from: http://nhutchpersonalgeog.weebly.com/1-where-commuters-run-over-black-children.html Maps do more than just help us explore - maps can tell very powerful stories. This infamous map of downtown Detroit from 1968 is called Where Commuters Run Over Black Children
from: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windsor_Sculpture_Park As I mentioned before I'm interested in how web mapping allow us to make our own maps You may have noticed that Wikipedia now have longitude and latitudes for many of its entries
from: http://inkdroid.org/ici/ Open data plus open standards means that new kinds of maps can be built This is a tool that tells you which things are near that have been geocoded in wikipedia

from: http://www.strava.com/ Here's another map bike trails in Windsor. Except the trails aren't drawn by hand These are trails made by the GPS tracks by those using the Strava app
from: http://www.openstreetmap.org/ Strava chooses not to buy the high licensing fees set by Google to make its maps It uses the Wikipedia of Maps called OpenStreetMap: I added most of the features of the UW campus with it
